## Background and Need

### Current Challenge

Santa Fe's homeless service ecosystem operates without coordinated information sharing:

- Providers don't know which other services individuals are receiving
- Duplication of intake and assessment processes
- Missed opportunities for rapid housing intervention
- No community-wide tracking of progress toward functional zero
- City lacks real-time data for decision-making

### Pilot Phase Success

Emergency contract funding ($20k increments) enabled:

- MVP specification developed with provider input
- Initial provider teams committed to participation
- HIPAA-compliant technical architecture designed
- Platform development initiated
- Trust-building with homeless services community

### Need for Sustainable Structure

To fully implement and sustain this system requires:

- Legal entity for HIPAA Business Associate Agreements
- Stable funding for operations and personnel
- Institutional accountability to providers and community
- Long-term commitment beyond emergency contracts

**Mitigation:**

- Enterprise-grade hosting infrastructure
- Regular backups and disaster recovery plan
- Redundant systems and failover capability
- 24/7 monitoring and alerting
- Experienced technical team

### Risk: HIPAA Violation or Data Breach

**Mitigation:**

- BAAs with all technology vendors
- Comprehensive security safeguards
- Regular security audits
- Staff training on HIPAA compliance
- Incident response plan

### Risk: Funding Sustainability Beyond Year 1
