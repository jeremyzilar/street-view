## Common Sticking Points

Issues that come up frequently - be ready for them:

### "That admin fee seems high..."

**Response:** The 8-12% covers critical infrastructure you can't provide as an individual:

- Legal entity to sign HIPAA BAAs
- Insurance ($10K+ value)
- Financial management and reporting
- Nonprofit status for grants
- Institutional accountability

It's not overhead - it's essential capacity.

---

### "How much control will you have vs. the sponsor?"

**Response:** Model C sponsorship means significant operational independence:

- You: Day-to-day decisions, provider relationships, technical choices, spending within budget
- Sponsor: Legal authority, major contracts, budget approval, compliance oversight
- Together: Strategy, major policy changes, hiring additional staff

Define this clearly in your fiscal sponsorship agreement.

---

### "What if it doesn't work out?"

**Response:** Fiscal sponsorship agreements include exit terms:

- Either party can terminate with notice (30-60 days typical)
- Clear process for transitioning data and operations
- Can "graduate" to independent nonprofit if appropriate
- Much cleaner than if you're just individual contractor

## Mindset for Success

### Remember:

**You're offering an opportunity.** Fiscal sponsors benefit from:

- Mission-aligned work that advances their goals
- Revenue from admin fees
- Positive visibility and innovation
- Manageable commitment (you handle operations)

**This is a partnership.** You need them, they benefit from you. It's mutual.

**Clarity builds trust.** The more clear you are about what you need and what you offer, the easier it is for them to say yes.

**Take your time with selection.** A good fiscal sponsor relationship can last years. A bad one causes constant friction. Choose carefully.

**Maintain your values.** You can uphold mutual aid principles within formal structure. The legal entity is infrastructure; how you operate determines values.
